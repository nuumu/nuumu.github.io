Massage Therapy

Massage Therapy is used for a variety of health benefits such as increasing relaxation, stress reduction, pain relief, and rehabilitation of sports injuries. To promote general wellness in the body, there are several types of massage techniques used to target various muscles and soft tissues.

Swedish

This classic full-body massage uses long strokes, kneading, deep circular movements, vibration, and tapping to reduce tension and soothe sore muscles. It increases circulation and promotes total relaxation.

Sports/Deep Tissue

This massage uses deep, deliberate strokes to separate and lengthen muscle fibers and focuses on myofascial trigger points (i.e. painful muscle "knots"). Great for muscular tension and spasms due to athletic activities, stress or overwork.

Shiatsu

This is massage using acupressure. Finger (shi) pressure (atsu) is used to release blockages along the bodyâ€™s energy pathways. It relieves tension and stress, restores balance, and renews energy.

Thai Yoga

This is a combination of Swedish and Thai massage. It uses yoga stretching and pressure point stimulation to restores energy, increase flexibility and enhance circulation, while soothing the digestive system.

Hot Stone

This unique form of massage uses hot and cool stones applied in various strokes. The heated stones penetrate into the muscles to relieve tightness and soreness, while the cool stones soothes and restores the body.

Aromatherapy

This popular massage uses the healing properties of pure therapeutic essential oils to enhance healing, relieve pain, and reduce stress. Several types of essential oils and custom blends are available.

Thai Herbal Ball

This is a special herbal massage which combines heat therapy, herbal therapy, and aromatherapy in one modality. A special blend of medicinal herbs (such as tamarind, kaffir leaves, lemongrass, shikakai, mangosteen, ginger) are put into a muslin cloth and tied into a ball. When steamed, the herbal ball releases the healing properties of the herbs inside. It alleviates muscle stiffness, promotes relaxation, relieves pain and inflammation, improves blood and lymph circulation, and can help certain skin conditions.

About

Jane Hill, CMT

Jane Hill, CMT is a Certified Massage Therapist providing massage therapy services in the greater Los Angeles area since 2003. Jane has over 1,000 hours of bodywork training and is fully certified in Swedish, Shiatsu, Deep Tissue, Trigger Point, Sports, Hot Stone, and Thai massage. She has worked in upscale spas, chiropractor clinics, and wellness centers. She offers an intuitive touch and her therapy has helped many people with pain relief and stress reduction.
